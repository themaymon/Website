Research

Ecologically valid simulation science.

My research program is driven by a single, overarching goal: to establish a rigorous science of human behavior in simulated environments.

For decades, psychology has faced a ‚Äúvalidity trade-off.‚Äù We could study behavior in the real world (high ecological validity, low control) or in the lab (low ecological validity, high control). 2. Emotion Regulation Under Threat

How do we regulate fear when the threat feels immediate?

Traditional labs study fear using static images on a computer screen. But looking at a picture of a spider is fundamentally different from standing on a ledge 80 floors up.

My ‚ÄúVirtual Plank‚Äù and ‚ÄúFilthy Bathroom‚Äù (Disgust) paradigms allow us to test: * Inhibitory Control: Does fear paralyze us or speed us up? Future Vision: The Simulation Science Lab

My long-term aim is to establish a dedicated Simulation Science Laboratory. This facility will move beyond simple HMD setups to fully integrated multi-modal simulation, combining:

  • Wireless EEG & Mobile fNIRS for neural imaging in motion.
  • Full-body Motion Capture for behavioral kinematics.
  • Haptic Feedback to ‚Äúclose the loop‚Äù of sensory immersion.

This lab will not just study VR; it will use VR as a tool to understand the fundamental mechanisms of the predictive brain.
